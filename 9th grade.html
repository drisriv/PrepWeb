9th Grade



General Advice

1. Meet with your counselor and discuss what classes you are interested in taking and whether you are eligible for them. Some schools may even offer opportunities such as dual enrollment which gives you the chance to start taking college courses as a high schooler.

2. Get involved in your school. This includes participating in clubs, sports, the student council, and other various extracurricular activities. Find what you are passionate about and join clubs related to your interest(s).

3. Volunteer around your community. Some schools or clubs may require students to aquire volunteer hours but besides volunteering just to get hours, you should volunteer to connect with your community. You'll gain new experience and skills as well as make meaningful memories and connections.

4. Get good grades. Freshman year is not the time to slack off! The grades you earn freshman year will still count towards your GPA

5. Challenge yourself by taking honors and AP classes, but don't take on more then you can handle. Know your limits and set goals for yourself.

What is Naviance?

Naviance is a college and career readiness software site that partners with high schools to provide students with college planning and career assessment tools. On Naviance, they have a scholarship search engine, a find your fit college search quiz, a resume builder, and much more. Naviance's features can help you better understand your learning styles, strengthen study and test taking skills, and create an academic plan to meet goals. Take advantage of this site!
