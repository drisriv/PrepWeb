10th Grade



General Advice

1. Think about starting your own club or aim to get a leadership position in a club you are already a part of.

2. Keep your grades up but also try to challenge yourself by taking honors or AP classes and maybe even dual enrollment (if your school offers).

3. Spend your summer wisely. There are many opportunities out there for you to explore things you may be interested in. For example, if you are interested in learning about coding, Kode with Klossy offers a free 2 week camp on topics such as web and app development.

4. Get ready for standardized tests. Most sophomores take the PSAT and then take it again during their junior year. These test results help determine what you need to work on to be ready for the SAT and the PSAT scores help to see if you qualify for a National Merit Scholarship.

5. Start visiting colleges and creating your college list.
